# Valor Sports Academy — Copy Audit & Rewrite (v2)

Goal: kill the AI-sounding patterns, sharpen the supporting copy, and talk straight to the parents who pay. Hero tagline stays (it's their brand). Coach bios stay (they wrote them).

---

## The 30-second verdict

The copy is clean and organized. The problem is it reads like good AI copy: smooth, balanced, a little hollow. Three patterns are doing most of the damage.

**1. The "X, not Y" reflex.** "Coached by players, not a clipboard." "A plan built for your body, not a template off a wall." "Real attention instead of getting lost in a crowd." "Football is one of our specialties, not the whole story." One is fine. Five on one site is the biggest tell you have.

**2. The rule-of-three rhythm.** Almost every sentence stacks things in tidy threes: "structure, confidence, and leadership." "The strength, the speed, the conditioning, and the mindset." That even hum is what people now read as machine-written.

**3. Filler words pretending to be proof.** "Real results." "Real attention." "Real plan." The word *real* is doing the job that a number or a name should do. Same with *actually*. Strip them and replace with something concrete: 25 kids max, three coaches, $90 a week, college-level coaches.

Fix those three and the site stops sounding generated and starts sounding like Valor.

---

## ⚠️ Accuracy flag: "three coaches per session"

This claim is not true, and it's repeated across the site, not just in one spot. It appears on:
- **Home** — hero support line and the "what we do" bullets
- **Programs** — "three coaches per session" (twice)
- **Youth Football** — "Classes capped at 25 with three coaches"
- **Sports Training** — "Classes capped at 25 with three coaches"

Find and replace it everywhere before launch. Either drop the coach count entirely ("Classes capped at 25 so every athlete gets coached") or state the real ratio. A false claim a parent can disprove on day one does more damage than any AI-sounding line.

---

## Voice rules to write by

- **No "X, not Y" constructions.** Say what you ARE. Drop the contrast.
- **Break the threes.** One specific thing, or two. Vary sentence length.
- **Ban "real" and "actually" as filler.** If you're tempted to write "real results," you're missing a number. Go find the number.
- **Lead with the concrete.** "25 kids max, 3 coaches" beats "real attention" every time.
- **Write like a coach talks to a parent at pickup.** Short. Plain. A little blunt.
- **No em dashes. No slogans.** "Built different," "championship habits," "win reps," "the four engines of speed." If a high schooler would roll their eyes, cut it.
- **Talk to the buyer.** On youth pages that's the PARENT. Their worry: is my kid safe, will they get better, am I wasting money, will they get attention.

## HOME PAGE

### Hero — KEEP the tagline. Fix one support line.

Leave **"Train With Purpose. Rise With Valor."** and the intro paragraph as-is. That's the brand.

The one thing to change is the support line under it:

**Current:** "Capped classes. Coaches who've competed. Real results."

**Problem:** "Real results" is the exact AI filler to avoid. It's three empty-ish beats, and the last one says nothing.

**Rewrite:** "Classes capped at 25, so your kid gets seen."

That swaps a vague claim for a fact a parent cares about. (Avoid the "three coaches" line here, see the accuracy flag below.)

### "Three ways to train" section

Headline is fine. Tighten the intro:

**Current:** "Pick the track that fits, from youth football to all-sport development to one-on-one work for adults. Every session is run by coaches who've competed."

**Rewrite:** "Three programs, one standard of coaching. Pick the one that fits your athlete."

Football card:
- **Current:** "Position-specific technique, fundamentals, and football IQ from coaches who play and teach the game at the high school and college level."
- **Rewrite:** "Position technique and football IQ, taught by coaches who play and coach the game at the high school and college level."

### "Built different / meets your athlete where they are" section

**Current headline:** "Coaching that meets your athlete where they are"
**Problem:** "meets you where you are" is a top-five corporate cliché. "Built different" is a borrowed meme.

**Rewrite headline:** "We coach the kid in front of us."
**Kicker (replace "Built different"):** "Richland's training home"

**Current body:**
> Valor trains athletes of every age and every sport... The work is simple and it's hard: build the strength, the speed, the conditioning, and the mindset to compete. Then do it again the next day.

**Problem:** "simple and it's hard" is a paradox cliché, the four-item list is the three-rhythm tell, and "Then do it again the next day" is a punchline fragment.

**Rewrite:**
> We train kids of every age and every sport, from grade-schoolers up to adults working one-on-one. Football is a specialty, but most of what we do is build the base every athlete needs: get strong, get fast, last all game. It's not complicated. It's hard work, done over and over.

The three bullets underneath are the most concrete thing on the page. Keep two, fix one:
- Coaches with college and high school experience
- Classes capped at 25, three coaches per session → **the "three coaches" part isn't accurate. Fix it.** Use "Classes capped at 25 with hands-on coaching" or whatever the true coach-to-athlete number is.
- Affordable training built for Tri-Cities families
